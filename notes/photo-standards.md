# Product Photo Standards — Zita Boutique (Canva workflow)

How to prep supplier/dropship photos in **Canva** so a catalogue of mixed sources
still looks like one clean store.

## How the site uses your photo (so the rules make sense)

- **Grid tiles** (home feed, category grid, "More in this category") crop your photo
  to a **3:4 portrait** and fill it — a centred crop, edges trimmed.
- The **product page** shows the fuller photo in a swipe carousel.
- The **first photo** of a product is the one shown everywhere — grid thumbnail, bag,
  shared link. Make it the strongest.

So the golden rule: **frame the garment centred, head-to-hem, with a little room top
and bottom.** Then every crop the site makes stays clean.

## One-time Canva setup: a 3:4 template

1. Canva → **Create a design → Custom size → 1500 × 2000 px** → name it "Zita product".
2. Keep this design and reuse it — replace the photo each time, or duplicate the page
   per product. 1500 × 2000 is sharp on phones and zoom without being heavy.

## Per-photo steps in Canva

1. **Drop the supplier photo in and crop to fill** the 1500 × 2000 frame (drag the
   corners). Keep the garment centred, whole (head-to-hem or full flat-lay).
2. **Remove watermarks / baked-in text** — supplier logos ("COCOLELO", "XY-star"),
   size charts ("S-5XL"), price stamps ("2001#", "-9%"):
   - Near an edge → just **crop it out**.
   - Over the garment → **Edit image → Magic Eraser** (Canva Pro) → brush over it.
   - Can't clean it → **don't use that photo**, pick another angle.
3. **Clean the background** (makes the grid cohere) — **Edit image → BG Remover**
   (Canva Pro) → set the background to **white**. Do this at least for the first photo.
   No Pro? Then just prefer photos that already have a clean, plain background.
4. **Never use a multi-dress collage as the first photo.** If the supplier only gives a
   4-in-1 lineup, **crop one garment out** for the main photo; add the others as extra
   photos further down.
5. **Download** — Share → Download → **JPG**. Aim **under ~1.5 MB**; hard limit **10 MB**
   per file (a bigger file fails the whole product upload).

## Consistency rules

- **One background style across the whole catalogue** — white is safest.
- **Same framing and zoom** for every product in a category — don't mix full-body with
  waist-up; it makes the grid look ragged.
- Garment fills roughly **80–85%** of the frame.

## Colours

- For each colour, that colour's **first photo must actually show that colour**.
- Set the colour **swatch** to match the real garment: in Canva, open any colour
  picker, use the **eyedropper** to sample the garment, copy the hex code, and paste it
  into the colour's hex field in the admin. A swatch that disagrees with the photo looks
  like a bug.

## Master specs (recap)

- **3:4 portrait, 1500 × 2000 px**, JPG, **under ~1.5 MB** (max 10 MB per file).
- **Reject:** foreign watermarks, baked-in text/size charts, multi-dress collages as the
  main photo, and anything blurry or smaller than ~1000 px wide.

## Why this shape

Supplier photos come in every ratio, background and watermark. The site crops thumbnails
to a uniform 3:4 so the grid reads as one designed store rather than a marketplace dump —
your Canva prep is what makes that crop land on a clean, centred, watermark-free garment.
